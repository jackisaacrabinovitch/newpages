Welcome!

I am Jack Isaac Rabinovitch [dʒæk ˈaɪzək ɹæˈbɪnoʊvɪtʃ], I am currently a third year PhD student at the Department of Linguistics at Harvard University, as well as a lab member at the Meaning and Modality Lab. I also run and organize the Harvard Manchu Reading Group!

I am fascinated by the dichotomy of signals (speech, signing, writing, art, cultural practices, etc.) both as tools for description and demonstration of our thoughts as well as independent systems governed by cognitive structures which are themselves subject to description and demonstration. This fascination has led me to work on various projects including work on phonologically conditioned syntax in Classical Chinese and work on quotation, complementation, and indexical shift in Uyghur, Manchu, and other languages.

In addition to theoretical work, I also do documentation and conservation work on Manchu and Sibe language and culture. This includes running the Harvard Manchu Reading Group and the Sibe Grammar Wiki, a site where learners of Sibe can come to find information on particular grammar points.

In addition to my research, I also like to listen to music, make art, hike, bike, and take care of plants. Feel free to take a look at more about my personal life.
