Linguistics 97R: Writing Systems and Language Conservation

Hello Ling 97R Students! Here is the syllabus and all the handouts and assignments so far:

[Syllabus]

[Week 1 Handout] Where we discuss the question: What is writing? And some terminology we can use to dissect this question.

[Week 1 Assignment] Some field work where you find instances of non-Latin scripts, and a challenge using a "writing system" commonly seen on the internet.

[Week 2 Handout 1] Where we go over the invention of writing and the design of logographic scripts.

[Week 2 Handout 2] Where we discuss the adoption and evolution of writing systems.

[Week 2 Assignment] Making a rebus for your own names, some analytical work on some scripts.

[Week 3 Handout] Finishing writing system categorization with syllabaries and featural alphabets, and then moving on to how languages adapt non-native scripts and some strategies for representing sounds not found in the original script.

[Week 3 Assignment] Dealing with ambiguities and morphophonological considerations when designing an orthography.

[Week 4 Handout] Looking at Diasystems, different interactions between orthography and socio-politics and technology, and auxiliary systems.

[Week 4 Assignment] Looking at stress marking in Spanish and an English spelling rule you likely were not taught, but already know.

[Week 5 Handout] Looking at issues in orthography such as captialization, loanwords, and issues of power and accessibility in writing systems.

[Final Project] A preliminary analysis of Harvardese phonology and morphology in preparation for the new Orthographic Committee of the Harvard Archipelago (that's you!).
