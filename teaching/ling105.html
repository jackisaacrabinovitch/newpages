Linguistics 105: Sounds of Language

I was a teaching fellow for Linguistics 105: Sounds of Language, taught by Kevin Ryan. This entailed running two discussion sections each week, grading homeworks and tests, and holding office hours. Below I have archived my handouts for the discussion sections.

Archived Materials

Hello Ling 105 Students! Here are all the handouts for our discussion section so far:

[Handout 1: 2021-09-09] Where we discuss place of articulation and manner of articulation, as well as phonation, nasality, laterality, and sonority.

[Handout 2: 2021-09-16] Where we discuss aspiration, Voice Onset Time (VOT), (a short introduction into) allophony, and vowels and vowel qualities. We also played a "name game/forehead detective" game, feel free to use the pdf for the "nametags" if you want to print and play your own!

[Handout 3: 2021-09-23] Where we discuss the basic structure of a syllable, stress, common allophony in English (useful for narrow transcriptions) and more cross-linguistic variation in vowels.

[Handout 4: 2021-09-30] Where we discuss F0, formants, and what they mean for the vowel space, as well as voicing and VOT. We also practice some of our allophonic rule application.

[Handout 5: 2021-10-07] Where we discuss airflow Mechanisms, phonation types, and perceptual dispersion.

[Midterm Review Sheet: 2021-10-14] This is a compilation of some of the parts of the previous handouts with some additional visuals and spectrogram reading help. Additionally there is a short practice for the kinds of questions that will be on the midterm. I've included in a separate file the answer sheet for the review sheet practice.

[Handout 6: 2021-11-04] Where we go over how to formally posit rules with natural classes for sound change phenomena, as well as the different kinds of rule interactions (bleeding, feeding, counterbleeding, counterfeeding).

[Handout 7: 2021-11-11] Where we discuss syllable structure and syllabification (such as onset maximazation), as well as go over some brief phonotactic typology, the sonority sequencing principle, and the very basics of stress. I also gave some additional practice questions, for students who were interested.

[Handout 8: 2021-11-18] Where we discuss prosodic structures (segments, morae, syllables, feet), and how that affects stress placement in predictable stress languages. We also discuss tone and autosegmental theory, as well as scansion and how stress is used in poetry.

[Handout 9: 2021-12-02] Where we discuss metrics and poetics, specifically the structure of iambic pentameter and naturalness and markedness in rule derivation. I additionally discuss some "best practices" when devising purely phonological rules.

[Final Review Sheet: 2021-12-09] This is a compilation of some of the parts of the previous handouts (after the Midterm) in addition to some extra notes on things which have been tricky in the past. Good luck, and thank you for being such wonderful discussion sections!
